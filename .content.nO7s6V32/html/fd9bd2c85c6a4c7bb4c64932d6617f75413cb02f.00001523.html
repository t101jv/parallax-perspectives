The Climate Crisis Means the Wrong People Are Running the World

October 6, 2019 GlenAnderson Climate, Conservatism and Right Wing, Democracy, Electoral Reforms, Voting Rights (Also see Political Parties and Elections), Economics, Labor, Capitalism, Trade, Poverty (Also see Taxes), Energy Issues (Also see Oil, Climate, Environment), Environment, Examples of Parallax Views, Governmental Abuses, International Affairs (Click to see specific nations and regions), Oil (Also see Environment and Climate), Organizing & Activism (Also see Nonviolence, Outreach), Our Current Political Crisis (in addition to Trump and Trumpism), Outreach, Framing/Reframing (Also see Organizing & Activism), Political Parties and Elections (Also see Democracy ...), Science, Trump and Trumpism (Also see Our Current Political Crisis) 0

If ordinary people around the world had political and economic democracy, we never would have allowed the climate crisis to get to this extreme emergency:

• We would have paid attention to the science.
• We would have sharply restricted big business and other economic powers.
• We would have forced governments to take strong and timely action decades ago.

The fact that the climate crisis exists now proves that the U.S. and the world are existentially suffering from a lack of political and economic democracy. The following article discusses this:

The Climate Crisis Means the Wrong People Are Running the World

There is no democracy when you can’t do anything about the companies polluting your rivers and air. The unbridled power of the big corporations is despotic.
